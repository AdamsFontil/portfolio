My Coding Journey

My Coding Journey began around this time last year. I really didn't want to go back to school. I forgot the reason. I think it had something to do with me not being blue badge at the time so I wasn't eligigle for tuition reimbursement. I value education highly but I really feel like it should be free, so I want to wait until Amazon could pay for me. I also really just wanted a break from Accounting so I thought a gap semester might help and rejuvinate your boy.

So after I decided to forgo returning to school in the fall. I had a lot of time on my hand, and I was bored. So I picked up a few habits, started to learn, do some yoga, and meditate. I was really into reversing the strain Amazon was putting on my back and psyche. And I eventually started to learn Bookkeeping and even got certified but after doing that I was like hmm, what now, who's going to hire, should I start my own practice? I wasn't ready for any of those things. A couple weeks down the line I was just scrolling through Reddit and saw some people talking about Computer Science. I was dismissive at first but job about CS degrees were all over my feed.

Eventually the pay, flexibility, fulfillment, and challenge of the job that so many had commented on got to me. And I now wanted to be a programmer. I started learning from App Academy and I thought it was insanely, and the cirriculum was free so I loved that too. But eventually things got really hard, so I bounced. I tried CS50, and that was hard too so I left. And I tried to a MOOC that'd teach me python. And I did that for a while, but it too got too hard. I eventually ended up with The Odin Project and swore that I wouldn't quit. And I didn't. I learned alot from that course most of what I what I know and I'm forever grateful to them. Most of the skills that I've reported below I learned from them. I'm still not where i want to be, although I am proud of how far I've come I'm still trying to get better, so I've shifted to learning from Full Stack Open another amazing ressource, completely free where I'm plan on becoming a much better developer who's great both front and backend development. I honestly really like this field it's about constantly improving and creating things and that's really up my alley and there's no forcing it here, like I had to in school.
